Gaming Museum

Museum overview

  • The National Video Game Museum is 'the place to be' for everyone who wants to know (and experience) more about history, social and cultural side of video games. The museum late its visitors also experience the games of tomorrow. Gamers from young to old are welcome to play, discover, learn and experience to share online.

History

  • History

    A garage box in The Hague is the place where the adventure in 2008 started, with two friends Hasan Tasdemir and Pascal Rappailles. The garage box quickly became too small, after which they moved to a shed in an industrial area. Unfortunately, the shed was not heated and had to transfer everything to one in the middle of winter vacant Ministry of Agriculture. Then this building was also emptied became the then collection of 10 arcade video games with some spare parts stored in an old one showroom for classic cars. Because there was no room for it to renovate or play cabinets, suitable ones were looked for room.

    • History

      Soon there were more people who wanted to join, so both the location if the hobby approach is no longer sufficient were, in addition to everyone's daily work. The initiative was imminent ruin her success. That's why the friends decided that a professional approach was necessary and it could no longer be continued as a hobby. In consultation it was decided that one of the friends, Hasan Tasdemir, would trying to develop the concept into a business. At which the others would advise and assist him in their spare time to support.
